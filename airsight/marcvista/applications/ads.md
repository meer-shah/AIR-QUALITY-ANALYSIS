# Ads

## The principle — the 3-second hallway test

An ad has 3 seconds. If a founder scrolling past doesn't get the one idea in 3 seconds, the ad failed. One claim, one accent, one action. Cut everything else.

## Static sizes

| Surface | Size |
| --- | --- |
| Instagram feed | 1080×1080 (1:1) / 1080×1350 (4:5) |
| Instagram story | 1080×1920 (9:16) |
| LinkedIn feed | 1200×1200 |
| LinkedIn sponsored | 1200×627 |
| Google display | 300×250, 728×90, 160×600 |
| Newsletter | 600×wide |

## Composition — four elements, no more

1. **Headline** — the one claim, sentence case, display weight. ≤8 words.
2. **Subhead** (optional) — one line of proof or specificity.
3. **CTA** — sharp violet button or text: "Book a free positioning call".
4. **Mark** — in a corner. That's it.

Whitespace is the fifth element. Don't fill it.

## Backgrounds — pick ONE per ad

- Paper `#FFFFFF` with black headline + violet accent (default, highest contrast).
- `brand/900` violet with white headline.
- Black with white headline + violet accent.
- An abstract geometric / mark-motif composition (never a stock photo).

## What's never in an ad

Stock people · more than one accent color · gradients-over-everything · 3+ lines of body copy · multiple CTAs · emoji · Title Case · hype words · busy backgrounds behind text.

## Example scripts

**LinkedIn feed (paper):**
> Headline: A mid product with sharp branding wins. *(branding in violet)*
> Sub: We fix your positioning first, then build around it.
> CTA: Book a free positioning call · mark bottom-right.

**Instagram story (brand/900):**
> Headline (white): Your product is good. Can the market tell?
> CTA: Tap to book a call · mark top-left.

**Google display 300×250 (black):**
> Headline: Brand sharp. Ship fast.
> CTA: marcvista.com · violet button.

## Short-form video (15s)

- **Structure:** Hook (0–2s, the claim on screen) → 2–3 quick proof beats (named clients / "100K+ hours") → CTA card (mark + "Book a free positioning call").
- **Production rules:** sharp cuts, Inter type cards, violet accent, one idea per card, no music-driven montage, no stock b-roll of people. Captions always on. Respect that most viewers watch muted — the message lives in the text.

## Paid copy voice

Cut 40% harder than the homepage. Paid is the most ruthless register: one claim, one CTA, no warm-up. Still MarcVista's rules — specific, no hype, sentence case.

## What success looks like on paid

A scroller understands "brand-first studio for founders" and one reason to care, in 3 seconds, and knows the next step is a free call. Clarity is the metric — not cleverness.

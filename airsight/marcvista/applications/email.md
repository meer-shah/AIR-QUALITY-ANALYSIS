# Email

Two kinds, two tones: **marketing** (a one-to-one note from the studio) and **transactional** (fast, factual, zero personality).

## Technical

- **Width:** 600px, single column, centered on `paper`.
- **Fonts:** Inter via web font with a system fallback (`Arial, sans-serif`) — email clients are unreliable, so the fallback must look fine.
- **Dark mode:** test it. Use true black/white-safe colors; don't rely on the violet for legibility of text.
- **Radius:** sharp (0px) buttons and panels, on brand.
- **Images:** always have alt text; never put critical copy inside an image.

## Marketing email structure

1. **Header:** small `lockup-horizontal` on paper, left-aligned. No giant banner.
2. **Body:** sounds like a personal note. Open with a specific line, not "We hope this finds you well." Short paragraphs, one idea, one CTA (a sharp violet button or a text link).
3. **Footer:** one-line boilerplate, contact (info@marcvista.com), unsubscribe, "© 2026 MarcVista" in `caption`.

Voice: MarcVista register, warm end of it. Like the founder emailing one person — because that's how it should read.

## Subject lines

- Under 40 characters. Specific. No emoji. No "🚀 Unlock…".
- ✅ "Your brand vs. your product" · "A free positioning call?" · "The two-team tax"
- ❌ "Newsletter #7" · "Big news inside!!!"

## Pre-header

The 35–90 chars after the subject in the inbox preview. Use it — extend the subject, don't repeat it. ("Most founders' sites don't sound like their product. Here's the fix.")

## Transactional email (very different tone)

Clear, immediate, no marketing voice. "Your call is booked for Thursday 3pm. Here's the link. Reply to reschedule." One action, no upsell, no personality. Sharp button, plain footer.

## Types we send

- Positioning-call confirmations + reminders (transactional).
- A short founder note when there's a real story or result to share (marketing, rare, valuable).
- Proposal / follow-up after a call (MarcVista register, specific to that founder).

## What we never send

Mass "newsletter" blasts with no point · emoji-stuffed subject lines · hype copy · stock-photo headers · more than one CTA in a marketing email · anything that sounds automated when it should sound personal.

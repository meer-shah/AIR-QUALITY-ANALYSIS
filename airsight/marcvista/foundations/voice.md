# Voice

> Aligned to the official brand book (Personality, Voice & Tone, Messaging). MarcVista's archetype is **The Mentor** — a trusted guide who's walked the path.

MarcVista runs **two voice registers** over one visual system:

- **Register 1 — MarcVista (marketing/advisory):** The Mentor talking to a founder. Thoughtful, practical, honest, founder-fluent. Used on the website, decks, proposals, cold email, company social.
- **Register 2 — Founder / LinkedIn (Shahmeer):** witty, first-person, self-aware. The human behind MarcVista. Used on personal LinkedIn posts, threads, comment replies. (Full personal system lives in `shahmeer-design-system/`.)

Both obey the rules below. Neither uses hype or agency-speak.

## The 4 core personality traits

Every piece of communication should reflect these. If something feels off-brand, check it against these four.

1. **Thoughtful** — Think before acting. Listen more than you talk. Diagnose before prescribing. Ask good questions first; explain the thinking, not just the conclusion.
2. **Practical** — Care about real results. Outcomes over deliverables. Give clear next steps. Cut anything that doesn't improve the founder's clarity.
3. **Honest** — Tell the truth, especially when uncomfortable. Honest feedback is the most valuable thing we offer. Admit what we don't know rather than bluffing.
4. **Founder-Fluent** — Speak founder language. Understand technical products, funding pressure, early-stage chaos. Connect every recommendation back to a business outcome.

## Voice characteristics

| Dimension | Our voice | Right | Wrong |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Vocabulary | Plain English, no jargon | "Positioning clarity" | "Strategic brand architecture optimization" |
| Sentences | Short, active, direct | "We help you clarify positioning." | "Positioning clarification is facilitated through our methodology." |
| Perspective | Speak to *you* directly | "Your positioning is unclear because…" | "Common positioning issues include…" |
| Attitude | Confident but humble | "Here's what we've learned about this…" | "The correct approach is always…" |

## The 3-second feeling test

In 3 seconds on a MarcVista surface, a founder should feel: *"This person has done this before, sees what I can't, and will tell me the truth."* Trustworthy and clear — not "wow, flashy."

## Tone by context

| Context | Register | How tone adjusts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Frustrated founder | MarcVista | More empathetic, slower. Understand before advising: "I hear you. This is genuinely confusing. Let's untangle it." |
| Celebrating a win | MarcVista | Enthusiastic, genuine joy: "This is exactly it. Here's why this clarity changes everything." |
| Giving hard feedback | MarcVista | Direct, factual, solution-first: "This messaging isn't working. Here's specifically why, and what to replace it with." |
| Teaching / sharing | MarcVista | Authoritative, lead with credibility: "We've seen this pattern across dozens of founders. Here's what consistently works." |
| Homepage / hero | MarcVista | Confident, declarative. The core message, then the proof. |
| Cold email | MarcVista | Lead with *their* problem (a specific positioning gap), not us. One clear next step. |
| Case study | MarcVista | Problem → what we clarified → the outcome. |
| Founder LinkedIn post | Founder | Witty, first-person, a real opinion. Earns the smile, then lands the point. |
| Comment replies | Founder | Quick, warm, human. |

## The core message (never deviate)

> **We clarify your brand identity first, then build your product around it.**

This goes in the website headline, cold emails, the pitch, the first conversation. Everything else supports it.

## Messaging pillars — lead with the one that matches their pain

1. **Avoid wasted runway** *(pre-product founders)* — "Most founders waste 3–6 months building for the wrong market. We get you clear in 2 weeks so you only build what matters." Frame: runway anxiety, money + time.
2. **Accelerate sales & fundraising** *(post-launch / Series-A prep)* — "Clear positioning directly impacts your ability to close deals and raise. Investors and customers need to instantly get why you're different." Frame: revenue, not brand theory.
3. **Founder confidence** *(first-time founders)* — "You'll know exactly who you serve and what problem you solve — and that clarity becomes your confidence when you pitch, hire, and sell." Frame: clarity cures imposter syndrome.

Always return to the core message.

## Grammar & house style

Sentence case (ALL CAPS only in a ≤3-word overline). Oxford comma. One space after periods. Numerals for numbers ("2 weeks", "$500K"). Em dashes sparingly. Contractions on (it's, you're, we've). No exclamation points on MarcVista marketing.

## When there's nothing specific to say

Don't reach for an adjective. Cut the sentence, or replace it with the concrete next step ("Book a free positioning call") or a real number. A short true line beats a long impressive one. **Clarity over cleverness.**
